1. What is Software Testing?
Your Answer

Software testing is the process of checking an application if it able to perform the features it is designed to perform or there are defects in it before deploying the application to the end users.

Score: 9/10 ✅

Improved Interview Answer

Software testing is the process of verifying and validating that a software application works as expected, meets business requirements, and identifying defects before it is released to end users.

Why?
Recruiters often listen for the terms verification, validation, and requirements.

3. What is SDLC?
Your Answer

SDLC is the stages or phases requires to build or develop a software.

Score: 9/10

Better wording:

SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle) is the structured process used to plan, design, develop, test, deploy, and maintain software.

4. What is STLC?
Your Answer

STLC is the phases requires in testing a software before deployment to end user.

Score: 9/10

Interview version:

STLC (Software Testing Life Cycle) is the structured process followed by the QA team to plan, design, execute, and close software testing activities.

5. Difference between SDLC and STLC
Your Answer

SDLC is basically done by the development team while STLC is done by the Qa team

Score: 7.5/10

This is partly correct, but interviewers usually expect a bit more.

A stronger answer:

SDLC	STLC
Focuses on developing the software	Focuses on testing the software
Covers the complete software development process	Covers only testing activities
Involves developers, testers, business analysts, and project managers	Primarily involves QA/test engineers
6. What is a software bug?
Your Answer

Software bug is the variance or decrepancies between actual result and expected results. It is a defect that stop software from performing its actual features.

Score: 9/10 

Excellent.

A polished version:

A software bug is a defect or error that causes the actual behavior of the software to differ from the expected behavior.

7. Explain Bug Life Cycle.
Your Answer

Bug Life cycle is the stages from which bug is detecting and fixed.

Score: 8/10

Expand it slightly:

The Bug Life Cycle describes the stages a defect goes through from the time it is reported until it is verified as fixed and closed. Common stages include New, Assigned, Open, Fixed, Retest, and Closed.
